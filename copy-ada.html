Augusta Ada king, countess of Lovelace was born Dec 10, 1815 and died Nov 27, 1852. Her parents were Lord Byron and Lady Byron. She was married to William King-Noel, 1st Earl of Lovelace. Her children were Anne Blunt, 15th Baroness Wentworth (daughter), Ralph King-Milbanke, 2nd Earl of Lovelace (son), Byron King-Noel, viscount Oakham (son).
She is considered the first Computer Programmer. She was because she discovered that the Analytical Engine could perform a large series of mathematical operations.
Ada Lovelace had her own special tutors. Then educated herself. She was helped in her higher studies by Augustus De Morgan, the first teacher of mathematics at the University of London. She attended the University of London to study Math, and logic direction.

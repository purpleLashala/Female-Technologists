Katherine Johnson

Katherine Johnson was born August 26th, 1918 and she died February 24th, 2020 Katherine's real name was Creola Katherine Coleman. Her parents were Joylet Roberta, Joshua McKinley Coleman; she was one of four children. Her children were constants, Joylette, and Katherine.

Katherine accomplished complex manual calculations and helped the use of computers to carry out tasks. She calculated trajectories, launch windows, emergency return pass for Project Mercury Space Flights, she calculated rendezvous and command modules for people who were going to the moon. Her calculations played a role in the development of the Space Shuttle Program. She worked on plans for a mission to mars.

She was awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom by President Obama. She got the Silver Snoopy Award and the NASA Group Achievement Award from Leland D. Melvin, an astronaut at NASA. The US Congress gave her the Congressional gold medal.

She went to high school in Institute, West Virginia even though her family lived in White Sulfur Springs. Because the school that was in White Sulfur Springs didn't allow black people to attend it after the 8th grade. After graduating high school, she got enrolled into West Virginia State University, (WVSC), a historically black college. When she went to school she took every course in mathematics that they had. Some new ones were added just for her. She graduated with Latin honors. She had degrees in Math and French. After college she became a teacher, when she married her first husband James Goble. She dropped her job and entered into a graduate Math Program. She was the first black woman to go to West Virginia University (WVU).

From 1958 until she retired. She was an aerospace technologist.
